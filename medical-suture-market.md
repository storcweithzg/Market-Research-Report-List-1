<p><p>The medical suture market is categorized into absorbable and non-absorbable sutures. Absorbable sutures are designed to dissolve over time, eliminating the need for removal; they are typically used in internal procedures where long-term support is not required. Non-absorbable sutures remain in the body indefinitely or until removed, ideal for external wounds or areas needing ongoing support. Each type serves distinct surgical applications, impacting clinician choice based on factors like healing time and patient needs.</p></p>
